# Coordinator Language Refinement

Status: Phase 16G-2 terminology review

---

## Purpose

Coordinator-visible language should reduce anxiety and make the next operational action clear.

Flag terms that sound punitive, regulatory-heavy, threatening, or surveillance-oriented.

---

## Terms To Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Prefer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| compliance failure | Sounds punitive | readiness issue |
| monitoring failure | Sounds threatening | prevention risk |
| violation | Creates anxiety | risk |
| noncompliant | Blame-oriented | needs review |
| surveillance | Implies being watched | observation for UX refinement |
| productivity | Implies evaluation | workload |
| score | Implies ranking | status |
| deficiency | Heavy regulatory tone | gap |
| escalation failure | Blame-oriented | escalation risk |
| audit problem | Anxiety-inducing | inspection readiness item |

---

## Preferred Language

Use:

- readiness
- continuity
- prevention
- recovery
- stabilization
- next action
- needs review
- can wait
- resolve before review

---

## Helper Text Style

Keep helper text short:

- what should happen next
- how to recover
- what blocks completion
- what can safely wait
- what risks escalation

Avoid explaining internal signal logic, runtime diagnostics, or observer interpretations in coordinator-facing copy.
