1.1.3 
Rationale 
The aim of Study [PROTOCOL_A004_AMEND_003] is to provide evidence of the effect of pentosan polysu[INVESTIGATIONAL_PRODUCT_A]te sodium 
(PPS) for treatment of pain and improvement of function in adult participants with knee osteoarthritis 
(OA) pain. 
The study will use a randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multi-centre design. Efficacy 
outcomes for [PROTOCOL_A004_AMEND_003] will contribute further evidence for PPS as a potential treatment for knee 
OA pain. Previous findings from randomised, placebo-controlled studies for knee OA symptoms 
performed by the Sponsor (PARA_005 and PARA_OA_008) suggest PPS treatment may have clinically 
meaningful outcomes in terms of pain and function. 
Results from PARA_005 (n=124) and PARA_OA_008 (n=61) have demonstrated that PPS is efficacious 
for pain reduction and improvement of function in participants with OA with moderate to severe knee 
pain. Although the maximum PPS treatment effect (pain reduction) generally occurred at Day 56, a 
clinically meaningful treatment effect was maintained through to Day 365 for most pain reduction 
endpoints, and the change from baseline (CfB) was always higher for the PPS group than placebo.  
In PARA_OA_005 (PPS 2 mg/kg twice weekly for 6 weeks) using the Knee Injury and Osteoarthritis 
Outcome Score (KOOS) pain scale, a ≥50% reduction in pain was reported in 42% of participants at 
Day 53 and in 30% at Day 165 for the PPS group, compared with 31% at Day 53 and 16% at Day 165 for 
the placebo group. A similar durable treatment effect on function was observed using the KOOS function 
scale. Using the numeric rating scale (NRS) pain scale, a ≥50% reduction in pain was reported in 40% of 
participants at Day 53, in 28% at Day 109, and in 15% at Day 165 for the PPS group, compared with 
25% at Day 53, 21% at Day 109, and 16% at Day 165 for the placebo group. 
PARA_OA_008 demonstrated improvements in the PPS 2 mg/kg ideal body weight (IBW) twice -weekly 
for 6 weeks group, measured by adjusted mean percentage change from baseline (%CfB) using a mixed 
model for repeated measures (MMRM). Improvements were seen in Western Ontario and McMaster 
Universities Osteoarthritis Index (WOMAC®) pain (p=0.045), function (p=0.017), stiffness (p=0.057), 
and overall (p=0.022) at Day 56. The proportions of participants achieving a ≥30% improvement in the 
WOMAC pain subscale score by Day 56 and Day 168 were 73% and 60%, respectively in the PPS

Overall Design 
[PROTOCOL_A004_AMEND_003] is a randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multi-centre study that will evaluate

The purpose of this study is to measure the change in pain and function with subcutaneous injections of 
pentosan polysu[INVESTIGATIONAL_PRODUCT_A]te sodium (PPS) compared with subcutaneous injections of placebo in participants with 
knee OA pain. Study details include: 
• 
The study duration will be up to 64 weeks. 
• 
The treatment duration will be 6 weeks. 
• 
The visit frequency will be twice weekly during treatment. 
• 
The visit/contact frequency will be every 4–6 weeks during the 52-week Follow-up period.

Number of Participants 
Approximately 466 participants will be enrolled into this study (Section 9.5).

Trial Population 
Participants must be 18 years of age or older, with a clinical diagnosis of OA of the knee, suffering 
moderate to severe knee pain, unresponsive to conservative, non-pharmacologic and pharmacologic 
therapy, and willing to stop treatment with all other systemic pain medications for the duration of the trial.

INTRODUCTION 
Pentosan polysu[INVESTIGATIONAL_PRODUCT_A]te sodium (PPS) is a semi-synthetic macromolecular carbohydrate derivative that 
resembles glycosaminoglycans. [SPONSOR_A] is developing PPS for the treatment of pain and the 
improvement of function in knee OA. 
Pentosan polysu[INVESTIGATIONAL_PRODUCT_A]te sodium (PPS) has been widely used for several decades in human and veterinary 
medical practice because of its anti-inflammatory and regenerative properties 
(bene-Arzneimittel GmbH, 2018 [1])(bene-Arzneimittel GmbH, 2018 [1]) and fibrinolytic, lipolytic, and 
anticoagulant properties. Marketed PPS products include Elmiron®, 100 mg oral capsules (European 
Medicines Agency, 2017 [2]) (Janssen Pharmaceuticals, 2020 [3])(European Medicines Agency, 2017 
[2]) (Janssen Pharmaceuticals, 2020 [3]) for the relief of bladder pain or discomfort associated with 
interstitial cystitis, approved for use in the European Union (EU), United States of America (US), and 
other markets, and injectable PPS products for the treatment of peripheral arterial occlusive disease and 
thromboembolic prophylaxis, approved for use in Italy (Fibrase®). In addition, PPS has been used for over 
20 years in the EU, Australia, and Canada, and since 2022 in the US to treat dogs and horses with OA, 
and joint lameness and stiffness (e.g.,. Cartrophen® Vet). 
2.1 
Study Rationale 
The aim of Study [PROTOCOL_A004_AMEND_003] is to provide evidence of effect of PPS for treatment of pain 
and improvement of function in adult participants with knee OA pain. 
The study will use a randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multi-centre design. Efficacy 
outcomes for [PROTOCOL_A004_AMEND_003] will contribute further evidence for PPS as a potential treatment for knee 
OA pain; previous findings from randomised, placebo-controlled studies for knee OA symptoms 
performed by the Sponsor (PARA_005 and PARA_OA_008) suggested PPS treatment may have 
clinically meaningful outcomes in terms of pain and function. 
Results from PARA_005 (n=126) and PARA_OA_008 (n=61) have demonstrated that PPS is efficacious 
for pain reduction and improvement of function in participants with OA with moderate to severe knee 
pain. Although the maximum PPS treatment effect (pain reduction) generally occurred at Day 56, a 
clinically meaningful treatment effect was maintained through Day to 365 for most pain reduction 
endpoints, and the CfB was always higher for the PPS group than placebo.

Day 53 and in 30% at Day 165 for the PPS group, compared with 31% at Day 53 and 16% at Day 165 for 
the placebo group. A similar durable treatment effect on function was observed using the KOOS function 
scale. Using the NRS pain scale, a ≥50% reduction in pain was reported in 40% of participants at Day 53, 
in 28% at Day 109, and in 15% at Day 165 for the PPS group, compared with 25% at Day 53, 21% at 
Day 109, and 16% at Day 165 for the placebo group.

2.3.1 
Risk Assessment 
Pentosan polysu[INVESTIGATIONAL_PRODUCT_A]te sodium (PPS) has been well tolerated in the reported controlled studies evaluating 
the 2 mg/kg twice-weekly dose regimen. The most common AEs were injection site reactions. In 
controlled studies evaluating parenteral PPS for OA, thrombocytopenia consistent with heparin-induced 
thrombocytopenia (HIT) has been observed at a frequency of 1–22%. HIT has been previously reported 
with PPS treatment (Pluda et al., 1993 [15]) (Swain et al., 1995 [16])(Pluda et al., 1993 [15]) (Swain et 
al., 1995 [16]). The observed and potential risks of study participation may include those associated with 
exposure to PPS, i.e.,. injection site reaction, increased liver enzymes, thrombocytopenia, thrombosis, 
allergic reaction, haemorrhage/increased risk of bleeding, adrenal insufficiency, pigmentary maculopathy, 
and those associated with medical evaluation and study procedures including venepuncture, MRI and X-
ray (Table 2-1Table 2-1). 
2.3.2 
Benefit Assessment 
The potential benefits of study participation are that participants with OA may experience reduced pain 
with improved physical function and QoL because of treatment with PPS. 
2.3.3 
Overall Risk-Benefit-Risk Conclusion 
The most reported AE is injection site reaction. Bruising, erythema, pruritus, swelling, and pain have been 
commonly reported with SC injection of PPS for the treatment of OA. These events are largely mild to 
moderate in severity, nonserious, and transient. 
Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT) with or without thrombosis remains an AESI and has been 
identified by clinical and laboratory investigation. Withdrawal of the drug appears to mitigate against 
development of serious events such as thrombosis. Slight elevations in liver enzymes 
(alanine aminotransferase [ALT]) have been noted in the clinical development programs, not exceeding 
Grade 2 and not reaching clinical significance. The risks of other possible side effects are small, or the 
side effects anticipated are generally considered mild or transient in nature. The risk profile of PPS 
remains favourable for continued development. Considering the measures taken to minimise risk to study 
participants, the potential risks identified in association with PPS are outweighed by the anticipated 
benefits that may be afforded to participants with knee OA pain.

4.1.1 
Scientific Rationale for Study Design 
A double-blind, randomised design is considered the gold-standard and is appropriate for the evaluation 
of the safety and efficacy of PPS in treating pain in participants with knee OA pain. The dose used in this 
study has been used in two previous Phase 2b studies, an Expanded Access Program (EAP) under 
IND (145312), and a managed access program for OA, and indicated beneficial results for pain and 
function. In addition, the use of a placebo control group allows the detection of a treatment effect. 
The inclusion of a rescue medication protocol provides access to pain relief in this group who would 
otherwise experience pain from their OA. 
Clinical outcomes will be assessed using validated patient-reported outcome measures of OA pain, 
function, stiffness, PGIC, and QoL (Section 8.1.1). 
The inclusion and exclusion criteria ensure that participants included in the study are those who 
experience OA pain despite long-term use of conservative therapy and may benefit from PPS treatment, 
while excluding participants who present with potential confounding factors, or for whom treatment with 
PPS would be contraindicated. 
4.2 
Justification for Dose 
Participants will be administered PPS 2 mg/kg twice weekly or placebo (normal saline) twice weekly for 
6 weeks. 
Various PPS doses and treatment durations have been evaluated in prior studies. The collective results 
establish a minimum effective dose and studies in non-OA indications suggest maximum tolerated doses. 
Phase 1 studies have demonstrated that after SC injection, systemic exposure to PPS is dose proportional 
after administration of single doses from 50 to 300 mg, multiple doses from 100 to 200 mg once weekly 
for 4 weeks, and 2 mg/kg once or twice weekly for 6 weeks. The doses discussed in this section fall 
within the dose range where dose proportionality has been demonstrated. 
4.2.1 
Studies With 2 mg/kg Twice-weeklyWeekly Dosing 
In 4 studies conducted by the Sponsor, the highest dose evaluated, 2 mg/kg total body weight twice 
weekly, has demonstrated treatment effects and established a safety profile. All 4 studies evaluated the 
safety of PPS following parenteral administration. Three of these studies (PARA_001, PARA_005, and 
PARA_OA_008) also evaluated the effect of PPS on pain reduction and improved joint function in 
patients with BMLs associated with anterior cruciate ligament injury and knee OA, respectively. In 
addition, the Sponsor has collected data from the use of PPS supplied under a Therapeutic Goods 
Administration (TGA) Special Access Scheme (SAS) in patients with knee OA and other similar 
conditions (hip, foot, hand) as well as in patients with knee OA in an EAP (PARA_EAP_OA_001) in the 
US. In general, doses of 2 mg/kg total body weight PPS were administered by SC injection twice weekly, 
for a duration of 3, 4, or 6 weeks, for a total of 6, 8, or 12 injections. Most patients received SC injections 
twice weekly for 6 weeks.

official unblinding of treatment assignment after database lock. No participant will be randomised into

8.1.1.2 
Western Ontario and McMaster Universities Osteoarthritis Index 
The WOMAC NRS 3.1 Index is a validated, self-administered, health status questionnaire that assesses

8.1.1.3 
EuroQOL Five-dimensionDimension Five-levelLevel Assessment 
The EQ-5D-5L questionnaire is a self-assessed, health related, QoL questionnaire consisting of the 
EQ-5D descriptive system and the EuroQOL visual analogue scale (EQ VAS.). The descriptive system 
comprises of five dimensions, where each dimension has 5 levels: no problems, slight problems, moderate 
problems, severe problems, and extreme problems. The patient is asked to indicate their health state by 
ticking the box next to the most appropriate statement in each of the five dimensions. This decision 
results in a 1-digit number that expresses the level selected for that dimension. The digits for the 
five dimensions can be combined into a 5-digit number. 
The EQ VAS records the patient’s self-rated health on a vertical VAS, where the endpoints are labelled 
‘The best health you can imagine’ and ‘The worst health you can imagine’. The VAS can be used as a 
quantitative measure of health outcome that reflects the patient’s own judgement. 
The EQ-5D-5L has been demonstrated to exhibit excellent psychometric properties across a broad range 
of populations, conditions and settings.

Statistical Hypotheses 
For CfB at Day 112 in knee pain as assessed by the weekly ADP subscale score NRS Index 
(11–point scale), the null hypothesis is that the mean change on PPS is greater than or equal to that of

The categories for classifying the Investigator’s opinion of the relationship are as follows:
Not related: 
Adverse event with an incompatible time relationship to IP administration, and 
that could be explained by underlying disease or other drugs or is 
incontrovertibly not related to the IP 
Possibly related: 
Adverse event with a reasonable time relationship to IP administration, but 
which also could be explained by concurrent disease or other medications 
Probably related: 
Adverse event with a reasonable time relationship to IP administration that is 
unlikely to be attributed to concurrent disease or other medications 
Definitely related: 
Adverse event with plausible time relationship to IP administration and which 
cannot be explained by concurrent disease or concomitant medications 
•
A reasonable possibility of a relationship conveys that there are facts, evidence, and/or arguments 
to suggest a causal relationship, rather than a relationship cannot be ruled out.
•
Alternative causes, such as underlying disease(s), concomitant therapy, and other risk factors, as 
well as the temporal relationship of the event to study intervention administration, will be 
considered and investigated.
•
The Investigator will also consult the IB and/or product information, for marketed products, in 
his/her assessment.
•
For each AE/SAE, the Investigator must document in the medical notes that he/she has reviewed 
the AE/SAE and has provided an assessment of causality.
•
There may be situations in which an SAE/AESI has occurred, and the Investigator has minimal 
information to include in the initial report to Sponsor and Safety Vendor. However, it is very 
important that the Investigator always make an assessment of causality for every event before the 
initial transmission of the SAE/AESI data to Sponsor and Safety Vendor.
•
The Investigator may change his/her opinion of causality in light of follow-up information and 
send an SAE/AESI follow-up report with the updated causality assessment.
•
The causality assessment is one of the criteria used when determining regulatory reporting 
requirements.
