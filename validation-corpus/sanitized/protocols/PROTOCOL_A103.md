2. Synopsis 
Protocol Name 
A Clinical Study Conducted to Evaluate the Performance of the 
CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test 
Protocol Number 
CS-1573-01 Version 1.0 
Intended Use 
The CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test is a lateral flow 
immunochromatographic assay intended for the qualitative detection of 
nucleocapsid protein antigen from SARS-CoV-2 in anterior nasal swab 
specimens directly collected from individuals aged 14 years or older or are 
collected by an adult from an individual aged 2 to 13 years of age. This test is 
intended for use in individuals with or without symptoms or other 
epidemiological reasons to suspect a COVID-19 infection.  
 
Results are for the identification of SARS-CoV-2 nucleocapsid protein antigen. 
The antigen is generally detectable in anterior nasal swab specimens during 
the acute phase of infection. Positive results indicate the presence of viral 
antigens, but clinical correlation with past medical history and other diagnostic 
information is necessary to determine infection status. Positive results do not 
rule out bacterial infection or co-infection with other viruses and the agent 
detected may not be the definite cause of disease. Individuals who test positive 
with the CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test should self-isolate and 
seek follow-up care with their physician or healthcare provider. 
 
Negative results should be treated as presumptive and confirmation with a 
molecular assay for patient management may be performed if necessary. 
Negative results do not rule out SARS-CoV-2 infection and should not be used 
as the sole basis for treatment or patient management decisions, including 
infection control decisions. Negative results should be considered in the 
context of an individual’s recent exposures, history, and the presence of clinical 
signs and symptoms consistent with COVID-19. Additional confirmatory testing 
with a molecular test for positive results may also be necessary if there is a low 
likelihood of COVID-19 infection, such as in individuals without known 
exposure to COVID-19 or residing in communities with low prevalence of 
infection. Individuals who test negative and continue to experience COVID-like 
symptoms of fever, cough, and/or shortness of breath may still have SARS-
CoV-2 infection and should seek follow-up care from their healthcare provider. 
 
Individuals should report their test result obtained with this product to their 
healthcare provider to receive appropriate medical care. All healthcare 
providers will report all test results they receive from individuals who use the 
authorized product to relevant public health authorities in accordance with 
local, state, and federal requirements using appropriate Logical Observation 
Identifiers Names and Codes (LOINC) and Systemized Nomenclature of 
Medicine (SNOMED) codes, as defined by the Laboratory In Vitro 
Diagnostics (LIVD) Test Code Mapping for SARS-CoV-2 Tests provided by 
the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

Number of Subjects 
Approximately 500 asymptomatic subjects will be enrolled into this 
study to obtain at least 15-20 RT-PCR confirmed SARS-CoV-2 positive 
asymptomatic subjects. 
 
The enrollment population for this study should represent different 
socioeconomic and educational backgrounds. Approximately 10% of 
the subjects enrolled will be from adults testing individuals aged 2-13 
years of age (minimum 50 subjects). 
Number of Sites 
Approximately five (5) geographically distinct clinical sites in the U.S. 
will participate in this study. In an effort to enroll subjects from diverse 
populations, study sites may include outpatient clinics, physician 
offices, urgent care clinics, emergency departments, research clinics or 
COVID-19 testing centers.  
 
Each participating site will conduct this study in a simulated home use 
environment. All study activities will occur at the designated study sites 
and IUOs will run the test per the provided Access Bio QRI. An eQRI 
(i.e., video instruction) may be used as a supplementary means for 
visual or auditory assistance. 
Study Duration 
Enrollment in this study is anticipated to last approximately three (3) 
months depending on the prevalence of COVID-19. 
Objectives of the Study 
The objective of this study is to evaluate the performance of the 
CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test in comparison to the FDA 
cleared Simplexa™ COVID-19 Direct test (K212147) or another FDA 
cleared assay. 
 
This study will focus solely on Arm 2 of CS-1518-01, with the aim of 
consolidating the performance data from this study (CS-1573-01) with 
the data from CS-1518-01 to support an FDA 510(k) or de novo 
regulatory submission.

Study Procedures 
This is a prospective multi-center clinical performance study to evaluate 
the sensitivity and specificity of the CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen 
Home Test when a lay person conducts the test on themselves, or 
another study participant, as compared to an FDA cleared high 
sensitivity RT-PCR comparator test. 
 
A subject’s participation in this study will consist of a single visit. 
Asymptomatic subjects seeking COVID-19 testing will be recruited by 
the site staff.  Following completion of the informed consent process 
and a review of Inclusion/Exclusion criteria to determine eligibility for 
study participation, each subject will receive a unique study 
identification number.

inquiries regarding the presence or absence of symptoms consistent 
with COVID-19 on the day of the visit and COVID-19 vaccination status 
(i.e., vaccine manufacturer, number of doses and date of last dose).  
Demographic questions (including age, sex, race, ethnicity, education 
level, and socioeconomic background) will be recorded. Pregnancy 
status and information on chronic health conditions will also be 
documented.

4. Investigational Device  
4.1. 
Name and Description of the Investigational Product 
The CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test is a lateral flow immunochromatographic assay for 
the qualitative detection of the nucleocapsid protein antigens from SARS-CoV-2. The test is designed 
to be read visually by detecting single or double bands of visible color. 
  
Nasal swab samples are loaded into the sample port of the test cassette, and extraction reagent is 
added. When the concentration of SARS-CoV-2 antigen in the specimen is above the minimum limit 
of detection (LOD), the viral antigen form complexes with labeled antibodies. The complexes move 
forward along the nitrocellulose membrane via chromatography until captured by pre-coated 
monoclonal antibodies specific for SARS-CoV-2 located in the detection zone (T) of the nitrocellulose 
film. The appearance of a blue reaction line in the detection zone (T), indicates the result is positive. 
Conversely, if there is no SARS-CoV-2 antigen present or the concentration of SARS-CoV-2 antigen 
in the specimen is below the minimum LOD, no blue reaction line appears in the detection zone (T) 
indicating a negative result.

6. 
Investigational Plan 
6.1. 
Study Design 
This is a prospective multi-center clinical performance study to evaluate the sensitivity and specificity of 
the CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test when a lay person conducts the test on themselves, 
or another study participant, as compared to an FDA cleared high sensitivity RT-PCR comparator test. 
A subject’s participation in this study will consist of a single visit. Asymptomatic subjects seeking COVID-
19 testing will be recruited by the site staff.  Following completion of the informed consent process and 
a review of Inclusion/Exclusion criteria to determine eligibility for study participation, each subject will 
receive a unique study identification number.   
Subject responses to relevant medical history questions including inquiries regarding the presence or 
absence of symptoms consistent with COVID-19 on the day of the visit and the date of onset of 
symptoms, and COVID-19 vaccination status including vaccine manufacturer, number of doses and 
date of last dose.  Demographic questions (including age, sex, race, ethnicity, education level, and 
socioeconomic background) will be recorded. Pregnancy status and information on chronic health 
conditions will be documented. 
Any swab specimens required for standard of care (SOC) testing will be collected prior to the study 
swabs. There will be a minimum fifteen (15) minute wait period between SOC swabs and both study 
swab collections.  
The reference sample (nasal swab) will be collected in a randomized fashion per the Site Operations 
Manual.  Odd numbered subjects will perform the CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test nasal 
collection a minimum of 15 minutes after the SOC swab. The reference sample swab will then be 
collected a minimum of 15 minutes after the CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test nasal 
collection. 
Even numbered subjects will have the reference swab taken by the study staff/HCP a minimum of 15 
minutes after the SOC swab. The subject will wait a minimum of 15 minutes and then perform the 
CareSuperb™ COVID-19 Antigen Home Test nasal collection and run the test. 
Each subject or parent/operator can either test themselves or one individual.  For example, a parent 
may either enroll themselves or enroll and run the test on one (1) child if they come in with multiple 
children, but they cannot do both. 
All reference samples will be stored and delivered to a central laboratory for reference testing according 
to the Site Operations Manual.  
All subjects will be provided their SOC results (where applicable) after the completion of CareSuperb™ 
COVID-19 Antigen Test testing and before leaving the site.  
The clinical study staff member will photograph each subject’s test result, labeled with their 
corresponding subject ID number, at the time each subject interprets their result. Additionally, the study 
staff member will record whether they agree with the test result’s interpretation by the subject, and if 
not, why. This information will not be used in analysis or change any subject’s interpretation of the test. 
It is included only for research purposes.
