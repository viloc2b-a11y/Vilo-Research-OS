1. INTRODUCTION 
ClinicaGen Bio has created a clinical partner network of non-profit health centers, independent 
medical groups, community-based clinics, hospitals, laboratories, and other healthcare organizations 
(“institutions”) capable of providing researchers with annotated biospecimens for use in biomarker 
discovery and validation, diagnostic test and instrumentation development, validation therapeutics 
development, and other medical research, including the impact that various specimen collection 
and handling methods and conditions have on results. This protocol covers the collection of 
biospecimens for research (either remnant specimens and/or those collected specifically for 
research) as well as any pre-screening and screening procedures required to determine which 
subjects meet eligibility criteria. 
 
The level of involvement for each network institution will vary based on the type of specimen to 
which they have access (e.g., biofluids, tissues and/or cells) and the category of collection (remnant 
specimens that were originally collected for clinical testing and/or specimens specifically collected 
for research) in accordance with the institution’s elected preferences. 
 
In most cases, potential subjects will be identified and approached upon presenting for clinical care 
or recruited specifically for the study using outreach programs and/or advertising. If additional pre-
screening activities are warranted to determine eligibility criteria, the potential subject may be 
presented with the opportunity to participate in these activities as part of the study. These pre-
screening activities will be minimal risk in nature and are described further below. Should 
potential subjects meet screening criteria, they may then be asked to provide biospecimens 
according to current research needs. Individual subjects or groups of subjects may be sought 
according to specific clinical, lifestyle, and/or demographic characteristics. The subjects providing 
these samples may be healthy subjects or subjects with a medical condition of interest to the 
research community but regardless, all specimens collected under this protocol (whether for 
screening purposes and for distribution to researchers) will qualify as minimal risk activities. 
 
Biospecimens may be distributed to researchers at academic institutions, hospitals, clinical and 
government laboratories, and corporations including but not limited to diagnostic, medical device, 
biopharmaceutical, and biotechnology companies. The types of research studies and testing that 
may be performed using the biospecimens will be varied, and it is not possible to provide a 
description of all potential types of use. Some researchers may perform genetic testing on the 
specimens, some may use the specimens to develop cell lines, and some may cryopreserve the 
specimens for many years, awaiting future research use. The ClinicaGen Bio consent forms will 
indicate a broad scope of possible research uses and activities. All specimens distributed to 
researchers by ClinicaGen Bio will be de-identified and the receiving facility will not be able to 
connect these specimens in any way to the subject who donated the specimen.  
 
If any of the research leads to new tests, drugs, or other commercial products, subjects will not 
share in any profits.

2. RATIONALE 
The development of products that diagnose, detect, treat, prevent, and cure complex human disease 
necessitates the use of biospecimens as a foundational component to all aspects of clinical research. 
This requires large numbers of affected and unaffected individuals to be appropriately identified by 
institutions with an understanding of the value the specimens have to the future of human 
healthcare. Moreover, during the past ten years, the drive towards precision medicine has 
accelerated the research community’s need for human biospecimens from a diverse, yet highly 
characterized, patient population.  
 
In vitro diagnostics, biotechnology, and pharma companies, as well as academic and public health 
entities, require millions of biospecimens annually. Today, they are procured through specimen 
brokers, biobanks, and medical centers from around the world with varying levels of both specimen 
quality and data quality and often are of limited quantity. A ready supply of quality biospecimens 
and associated data derived from medical, or research encounters are essential for clinical, 
academic, and commercial research to treat and prevent human disease. For example, to combat 
the pandemic of Covid-19 pharma required blood samples from previously infected individuals for 
rapid vaccine development, remnants from Standard of Care testing to track and identify variants 
has helped epidemiologists, serial samples of blood from the same patient over time has helped 
identify proper timeliness of vaccine boosters. Additionally, because minority patients have a 
historically low clinical trial participation proportion, minority populations may be more amenable 
to contributing minimal risk specimens, such as blood, in tandem with clinical care, thus increasing 
the potential to advance research representation in these populations. 
 
ClinicaGen Bio will not be engaged in implantable device trials. Biospecimens collected under this 
protocol are intended for research/development (pre-clinical) use by the Pharmaceutical or In Vitro 
Diagnostic device industry—near patient testing, point of care testing, and mod/high complexity 
laboratory instruments.  The 510k/PMA/EUA trials will not occur under this protocol, however data 
collected under this protocol may be compiled and used for regulatory purposes as required by 
Sponsor. 
 
While facilitating all important clinical endeavors requiring biospecimens, ClinicaGen Bio 
anticipates that implementation of the following protocol with IRB oversight across its network 
will also increase a patient’s ability and opportunity to make informed decisions related to the use 
of their specimens and allow traditionally underrepresented communities to experience clinical 
research. 
 
3. OBJECTIVE 
ClinicaGen Bio will facilitate obtaining and storing of biological specimens intended to be provided 
to researchers for use in diagnostic development and validation, therapeutics development, and 
other medical research. The specific source, type, and quantity of de-identified biospecimens and 
any associated clinical data will be communicated by ClinicaGen Bio to its network of associated 
institutions. The samples may also be used for genetic testing and include investigating the 
association between genes and their linkage to various disease indications or traits. This may 
include gene sequencing, gene-editing, and genotyping.

4. STUDY DESIGN 
4.1 Overview 
This multi-institution specimen collection protocol is intended to expedite proper collection and 
allocation of biospecimens for research objectives according to each institution’s medical forte 
and Standard of Care (ie dermatology, cardiology, infectious disease) and IRB approval. This 
protocol includes one or more of the following: 
• Clinical verification to confirm that the collection request is feasible for the network of 
ClinicaGen Bio sites.  
• Pre-screening activities and recruitment, such as medical chart review and/or health or 
lifestyle screening questionnaires to determine preliminary subject eligibility. 
• Screening activities that include direct patient involvement such as responding to a more 
detailed health or lifestyle questionnaire, a physical exam, vital signs, assessment of 
medical history, or specimen collection and testing. 
• Prospective collection of any specimens for research purposes only including “Research 
Use Only” or “RUO” or “Investigational Use Only” or “IUO” under minimal risk collection 
guidelines; and/or 
• Retention and distribution of remnant diagnostic specimens (“Remnant”) that are no 
longer needed for clinical testing or evaluation. 
 
4.2 Feasibility Evaluation 
ClinicaGen Bio will initiate feasibility activities related to collection requests by sponsors. 
Assessment of specimen type, anatomical collection source, logistics specific to medical 
specialty, remnant vs prospective collection, and other operational criteria will be evaluated. 
Collection requests that pose risk beyond the scope of this protocol will be declined. Once a 
request has been vetted and all necessary agreements reached, ClinicaGen Bio will notify 
network institutions participating in that collection type of the approved request, and the 
institutions will be submitted to the IRB as sites under this protocol. The specimens and 
associated data will be collected under this approved protocol.  
 
4.3 Pre-Screening, Screening and Recruitment Activities 
Pre-screening, screening and recruitment activities may consist of a medical record review and/or 
the pre-screening or screening of potential subjects over the telephone, via text message, via email, 
or in person to determine their initial eligibility for, and interest in, a biospecimen collection study. 
Pre-screening questions may consist of asking about medical and/or lifestyle information typically 
found in a medical record. All recruitment material will be submitted to the IRB for approval prior to 
its use including pre-screening questionnaires. 
 
Eligible subjects may be recruited in several ways. First, they may be approached in the 
institution’s relevant clinics by trained and appropriately delegated clinic/research staff, either 
with or without a pre-screening medical record review depending on the targeted specimen. 
Secondly, they may be recruited via outreach activities such as email, text messages or phone 
calls, or other programs, either with or without a pre-screening medical record review. In both 
cases, if they meet eligibility criteria based upon pre-screening and are interested in participating 
in the study, they will be provided with informed consent form(s) for any further screening 
activities, any RUO specimen collection, and/or any remnant specimen retention that is 
concurrent with prospectively collected samples.

4.4 Screening Activities 
Potential subjects will be further screened prior to any specimen collection. Screening activities may 
consist of data collection through non-invasive procedures that are routinely employed in a clinical 
practice (ie blood pressure), questionnaires (research-specific information that is not standard of 
care), and/or a minimal risk biospecimen collection for determination of eligibility testing. Subjects 
will be assessed for study eligibility through screening procedures. If eligibility criteria are met, the 
requirement for obtaining informed consent will be determined depending on the specimen 
required in the collection request (ie remnant versus prospective collection), and subject’s historic 
consent under this protocol. 
4.5 Remnant Clinical Specimen Use with Waiver of Informed Consent 
Remnant clinical specimens may include body fluids, surgical specimens, tissue biopsies, bone 
marrow, and cerebral spinal fluid that remain after the completion of diagnostic testing and may 
be distributed to researchers under this protocol. These specimens may be pre-existing or retained 
prospectively after use for medical purposes. 
 
Clinical sites may provide notification or disclosure to their patients that diagnostic remnants may 
be retained for research purposes, preferably in their HIPAA notification form. A waiver of 
informed consent is needed to maintain dates associated with the remnant specimens. The only 
foreseeable risk from allowing use of remnants in research is the potential loss of privacy or breach 
of confidentiality. 
 
4.6 Personal Health Information Usage 
All biospecimens will be collected and distributed with limited relevant personal health 
information, if requested, using either ClinicaGen Bio’s proprietary software or an existing 
institutional process. In either circumstance, direct identifiers will not be included with the 
collection once it has been prepared for distribution, except for dates of service. Each specimen 
retained will have unique identification codes generated that do not directly relate to patient 
identifiers. These codes will only be used to track the retained specimens associated with the 
institution.  Network institutions may send specimen and/or and data directly to ClinicaGen Bio. 
ClinicaGen Bio will only receive reports with PHI on them from network institutions in which there 
is a BAA in place and will act as the Honest Broker in those cases. No PHI (other than potentially 
limited data sets as described herein) is sent to the Sponsor. 
 
4.7 Genetic Research  
The samples collected under this protocol may be used for genetic research by the sponsor or any of 
the sponsor’s research partners or clients. The samples may be used for general genetic research, 
including, but not limited to, studying the relationship between genes and their linkage to diseases, for 
gene sequencing, gene-editing, and genotyping. Researchers have agreed not to make any attempts to 
identify the subjects. A potential subject may decline participation in the study due to the possibility of 
genetic research if they are so inclined. Subjects will have no access, nor will they be provided with, 
any of the investigational study results obtained from these samples. Subjects must not participate in 
this study to evaluate their health status.

8.0 RESEARCH PROCEDURES 
Each institution will be able to determine the specific specimen collection activities in which they 
will participate. Specimen collections may be provided for any verified legitimate research purpose, 
including genetic research. 
 
8.1 Software Automated Processes 
De-identification, annotation, handling and shipment of specimens may occur as part of an 
automated process using ClinicaGen Bio’s proprietary software or as a manual procedure 
according to institutional practice. 
 
8.2 Screening Procedures 
As part of screening, subjects may be asked questions about their medical history, 
lifestyle, family history, current illnesses, medications, and other relevant information. 
They may also undergo physical examinations, tests, or collections routinely employed in 
clinical practice, including the measurement of vital signs (heart rate, blood pressure, 
breathing, or temperature), physical exams, and minimally invasive or non-invasive 
collection of biological samples (blood, urine, salvia, or stool). A screening only blood 
sample will not exceed 50mls. 
 
8.3 Collection Criteria 
Once a subject has met any screening criteria, they may be asked to provide specimen(s). 
All specimen collections shall be performed using standard procedures for t h e  
collection, based upon the type of material being collected, and shall pose no more than 
minimal risk to the subject according to the standards found in the HHS and FDA 
regulations for expedited review of minimal risk research studies. For example, voided

urine, saliva obtained using either oral swabs or passive drool, and blood obtained using 
venipuncture, ear sticks, heel sticks or finger sticks, may be collected. Individual collection 
requests may specify unique requirements such as type of collection and site preparation,

requirements, storage requirements, labeling requirements, and data requirements.

collection requests may involve simultaneous multiple anatomical collections depending on the 
nature of the research (e.g. nasal swabs, and blood collection during a research visit for a Covid19 
work order).   
 
ClinicaGen Bio shall supply the investigator site with a Manual of Operations outlining all 
specimen collection, aliquoting, storage, and shipping requirements. The Manual of 
Operations shall be provided to the site prior to the initiation of any work order. All 
shipping details shall be included within the procedure, assuring that all specimens shall 
be packaged and labeled in compliance with applicable state, federal, and international 
regulations governing the transport of clinical specimens. 
 
To avoid any controversy, products of conception or aborted fetal tissue will not be collected under 
this protocol.

8.4 Remnant Specimen Collection Criteria-Waiver of Consent 
All remnant retention will be comprised of specimens that have been collected for clinical 
standard of care use (e.g. diagnostic sample) and would otherwise be discarded. Data 
associated with these specimens may contain remnant/patient-specific information that 
are not direct identifiers (e.g. collection date associated with date of service), therefore a 
waiver of consent is required. Remnants that are retained will have any identifiers 
removed (e.g. patient medical record ID) and will be relabeled with a coded ID number.  
 
8.5 Health Information 
8.5.1 Prospective   
Specimen collections may be annotated with relevant health data upon request by the 
Sponsor. Any such health information must be directly related to the aims of the 
proposed research and be the minimum necessary to accomplish these aims. This 
information may include basic demographic information, medical history, and other 
health records. Sensitive information, defined as anything that may reasonably place 
the subject’s financial standing, employability, insurability, or reputation at risk, will not 
be provided. All information will be provided in a manner consistent with the HIPAA 
Privacy Rule de-identification standards. 
8.5.2 Remnant  
Specimen collections may be annotated with relevant health data upon request by the 
Sponsor. Any such health information must be directly related to the aims of the

23.0  STUDY PROCEDURES 
Subjects considered for enrollment in this protocol shall be identified and approached by 
either the physician, or a qualified trained representative of the investigator site. The 
potential subject’s current medical records and the physician's diagnosis and instructed 
evaluation shall be used to evaluate whether the individual meets all inclusion and 
exclusion criteria as outlined within this protocol. Signed Informed Consent shall be 
obtained from the LAR and Assent if applicable. Neither the subject nor the LAR is to be 
unduly influenced to participate and shall do so willingly as evidenced by completion of the 
appropriate Assent Form and Informed Consent, respectively. 
Once enrolled into this study, all subjects participating shall have their biospecimen(s) 
collected. In some instances, subjects may be instructed to fast (no solid foods) for a 
certain number of hours prior to their sample collection. Most studies require only one 
study visit for collection of the biospecimen(s). In instances where fasting or more than one 
study visit or follow-up is required, subjects and/or their LAR shall be notified in advance of 
providing assent and/or informed consent. 
Biospecimen collections may include but are not limited to the following biospecimens: whole 
blood, dried blood spots (DBS), punch biopsies, fingernail clippings, plasma, serum, urine, saliva, 
mucus, other biofluids, stool and tissue specimens.

23.4 Tissue collections 
If tissue specimens are collected, they shall be obtained during a scheduled medical 
procedure from left-over tissues (i.e., surgical waste tissue), which are not required for 
diagnosis and would be otherwise disposed of. Surgical procedures shall not be performed 
on any subject for the specific purpose of obtaining specimens for research. 
 
23.5 Other biofluids 
Collection of other biofluids, including but not limited to peritoneal, cerebrospinal, or 
synovial fluid, may be obtained during a scheduled centesis procedure from left-over 
biofluids, which are not required for diagnosis and would be otherwise disposed. Centesis 
procedures shall not be performed on any subject for the specific purpose of obtaining 
specimens for research.
