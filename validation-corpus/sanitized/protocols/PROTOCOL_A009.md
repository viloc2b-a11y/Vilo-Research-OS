Protocol summary 
Study name 
Evaluation of MeMed BV Flex™ Performance and Ease of Use 
in a CLIA-Waived Clinical Environment  
Study Design  
This is a multi-center, prospective, sample collection study to 
evaluate the comparison between MeMed BV® test scores 
measured in a laboratory setting by trained operators using serum 
samples to MeMed BV Flex™ test scores measured at CLIA-
waived setting by untrained operators using either venous whole 
blood (VWB) or capillary whole blood (CWB) samples. 
The study will enroll patients over the age of 90 days who 
present with suspected acute bacterial or viral infection. 
Enrolled patients will be managed according to the current 
standard of care per institutional procedures. Study results will 
not influence patient management. 
Participation in the study requires the collection of up to 12 ml of 
blood. 
Study Population 
Patients over the age of 90 days, with clinical suspicion of acute 
bacterial or viral infection. 
No. of sites 
3-5 sites  
No. of subjects 
A total of approximately 220 patients will be enrolled in the 
study including approximately 40 pediatric patients. 
Study duration 
Approximately 6 months. 
Eligibility Criteria 
Inclusion Criteria: 
1. Written informed consent must be obtained from the 
patient or his/her legal guardian. For children under 18 
years old, written informed consent from the legal 
guardian and assent from the patient (depending on the 
local requirements). 
2. Over 90 days of age. 
3. Clinical suspicion of acute bacterial or viral infection.  
4. Symptoms of acute infectious disease < 7 days. 
5. Tactile fever or measured temperature ≥ 37.8°C (100°F) 
(anybody site, any measurement device, including self-
reported), either of them noted at least once within the last 
7 days.  
6. Collection of adequate volumes of capillary whole blood, 
venous whole blood and serum to support all required  
study assessments.

1. 
Introduction 
Antibiotic overuse, typically stemming from use to treat non-bacterial infections, has severe global 
health and economic consequences, including the emergence of antibiotic-resistant bacteria 2,3.

Antibiotic underuse due to delayed or missed diagnosis is also common (24%-40% of all bacterial 
infections) 4–7, and may result in prolonged disease state and medical complications 8–11. A major cause 
of antibiotic overuse and underuse is the difficulty of distinguishing between bacterial and non-bacterial 
(mostly viral) etiologies 4,11.  
1.1 The platform and test system:   
MeMed BV® is an in vitro diagnostic product including a dedicated disposable cartridge and an 
automated analyzer, MeMed Key®, which measures in parallel the blood concentrations of TRAIL, IP-
10 and CRP, three soluble non-microbial proteins that are generated naturally by the body and change 
expression in response to acute infection. The test result (score) is based on a combinatory signature of 
the three protein levels measured in patients suspected of acute bacterial or viral infection. The score 
ranges from 0 to 100 and is interpreted according to the following bins:  
• 0 ≤ score ≤ 10, high likelihood of viral infection (or other non-bacterial etiology); 
• 10 < score < 35, moderate likelihood of viral infection (or other non-bacterial etiology);  
• 35 ≤ score ≤ 65, equivocal;  
• 65 < score < 90, moderate likelihood of bacterial infection (including coinfection);  
• 90 ≤ score ≤ 100, high likelihood of bacterial infection 
The cleared test is compatible for use with serum and venous whole blood (VWB). 
The new test, MeMed BV Flex TM, which is the subject of this investigation, aims at lowering the blood 
volume required to conduct a test and enable testing of capillary whole blood (CWB) as well as VWB 
at CLIA-Waived or laboratory settings. The MeMed BV FlexTM test makes use of updated test 
cartridges running on the MeMed Key® analyzer using dedicated software and test workflows. The test 
measures the concentrations of the same proteins as the MeMed BV® (TRAIL, IP-10, and CRP) and 
employes the same computational algorithm to generate the score. Both tests are intended for use in 
conjunction with clinical assessments and other laboratory findings as an aid to differentiate bacterial 
from viral infection. 
MeMed BV® and MeMed BV FlexTM test systems consist of: 
• MeMed Key®: an automated liquid handling instrument comprising hardware (HW) and 
software (SW) that conducts chemiluminescence (CIL)-based analyte measurements and their

computational integration. The MeMed Key® analyzer is a small benchtop size device that at 
any given time can run a single test for a single patient.  
• MeMed BV® and MeMed BV FlexTM cartridge: a single-use per patient, multi-cavity container 
that receives the patient specimen, and contains all reagents necessary to conduct the TRAIL, 
IP-10 and CRP immunoassays. The cartridge also holds disposable tips employed by the 
analyzer and securely stores all waste materials collected during the test.  
• MeMed BV® and MeMed BV FlexTM calibrators and external controls: reagents with pre-known 
TRAIL, IP-10 and CRP concentrations, that are placed in the cartridges in the same manner as 
the patient specimen. The MeMed BV® and MeMed BV FlexTM calibrators are used to calibrate 
test cartridge on the MeMed Key® analyzer, whereas measurement of the control reagents serves 
to verify proper analyzer and cartridge functionality.

1.2 Study rationale 
The FDA 510(K)- cleared and CE marked, MeMed BV®, that will be used as the comparator method, 
is cleared for usage with either 100 µL of serum or 150µL VWB. MeMed BV® measures the blood 
concentrations of TRAIL, IP-10 and CRP, to aid physicians in distinguishing bacterial and viral 
infection. The subject of this investigation is a new test, MeMed BV FlexTM, which measures 
concentrations of the same proteins as MeMed BV® and uses the same computational algorithm 
employed to generate the MeMed BV score. MeMed BV FlexTM is designed to enable operators to 
obtain the MeMed BV score using a smaller sample volume, 50µL, of VWB or CWB.

The use of CWB, rather than serum or VWB, holds significant benefits to the healthcare provider as 
well as the patient, including a smaller blood sample volume taken from the patient in a minimal 
invasive procedure which can be performed faster and more easily.  
In addition, the new test is intended to be simple and easy to perform outside the clinical laboratory in 
CLIA-waived setting or by personnel with little to no experience running tests. This has significant 
potential to expand access to the MeMed BV test results for healthcare providers and their patients, 
enabling them to benefit from its availability during patient management. 
1.3 Potential risks and benefits 
1.3.1 Known potential risks 
Patients participating in the study do not receive any investigational drug or any experimental 
examination or procedure. The participants are exposed to the minimal risk associated with the 
collection of a venous blood sample (Phlebotomy) and a capillary blood sample. The risk of standard 
phlebotomy may include discomfort, pain, swelling, lightheadedness, fainting, infection or bruising 
which may be caused by venous rupture. To the greatest extent possible, the study-specific venous 
blood specimens will be drawn at the same time as blood is taken for routine care thus minimizing the 
risk associated with venipuncture. The risks of capillary blood sampling may include hematoma or 
scarring . 
These procedures are very common in clinical practice and are widely performed. The medical 
personnel who will perform the procedure are qualified and experienced. 
1.3.2 Known potential benefits 
Study participants are not expected to have any direct benefit following their enrollment to the study. 
Still, by participating in the study, participants contribute to the development of a new rapid diagnostic 
test.

An untrained operator is defined as a test operator in waived settings who has limited or no training or 
hands-on experience in conducting laboratory testing. 
The MeMed BV® test will be run using frozen serum samples. Serum will be processed, handled and 
frozen at the clinical sites according to the instructions for use contained within the study specific 
laboratory manual. Testing of the frozen samples will be executed by trained operators at a centralized 
location, outside of the clinical site. 
The MeMed BV Flex™ test will run by untrained operators according to the Quick Reference Guide, 
provided to the CLIA-waived sites, using fresh VWB and CWB. 
A total of approximately 220 eligible patients will be enrolled in the study including approximately 40 
pediatric patients.

samples will be run to establish a comparison between MeMed BV® and MeMed BV Flex™.  
Participation in the study requires the collection of up to 12 ml of venous and capillary blood in total. 
The following data will be extracted from the medical records for eligible patients and recorded in an 
anonymized case report form (CRF): demographics, medical history, concomitant treatment, clinical 
and laboratory information (e.g., CBC). 
Participation is based on patient’s free will and will take place following the signing of an informed 
consent form.  
Enrolled patients will be managed according to the current standard of care per institutional procedures. 
The test results will not be transferred to the treating physician and will not influence patient 
management.

Reference standard etiology was adjudicated by an expert panel based on clinical, laboratory, 
radiological, microbiological and follow-up data. Two separate adjudication processes were conducted 
to generate different reference standard cohorts as follows: 
 All-inclusive cohort: CRP, PCT, and BV score withheld from experts during adjudication and 
every case assigned a reference standard diagnosis (via a forced adjudication methodology).  
 Suspected cohort: only BV score was withheld from experts during adjudication and cases with 
indeterminate reference standard diagnoses were removed. 
In the Apollo study, the BV score was measured from serum samples. 
The first step in simulating the diagnostic accuracy of the MeMed BV FlexTM test will be to convert the 
serum measurements from the Apollo study to MeMed BV FlexTM measurements based on a simulation 
process. The simulation will allow for variability in the conversion based on the observed bias and 
coefficient of variation of the different analytes. Bias will be determined by the results of the [SPONSOR_A] 
study; the coefficient of variation (CV) will be calculated using data from (analytical validation) 
precision studies. The simulation will involve 100,000 runs to effectively reflect a gaussian distribution 
of possible results. 
The second step will be to compare the simulated MeMed BV FlexTM measurements generated for each 
run to the adjudication-based reference standard. This comparison will be conducted for both the all-
inclusive and suspected cohorts. 
The third step will be to assess if the diagnostic accuracy of the simulated MeMed BV FlexTM 
measurements pass pre-defined acceptance criteria. Diagnostic accuracy will be assessed by 
establishing that the probability of bacterial infection is an increasing function of the BV score. For this 
purpose, subjects will be assigned to score bins according to their simulated MeMed BV FlexTM score 
and within the bin according to their reference standard outcome: bacterial versus viral. The acceptance 
criteria are the same as used in the Apollo study: 
• The Cochran–Armitage (CA) Test for trend with a two-sided 5% level of significance will be 
used to reject the null hypothesis that there is no trend of increasing probability of bacterial 
infection with higher test score for at least 95% of simulations 
• Likelihood Ratio (LR) with 95% Confidence Interval (CI) should exclude 1 for some bins for 
at least 95% of simulations. 
95% confidence interval will be reported.

8.1 Proposed intended use/indication for use 
The MeMed BV FlexTM test is an automated semi-quantitative immunoassay that measures three non-
microbial (host) proteins (TRAIL, IP-10, and CRP) in adult and pediatric venous and capillary whole 
blood samples. The test is intended for use in conjunction with clinical assessments and other laboratory 
findings as an aid to differentiate bacterial from viral infection at Point-of-Care locations or clinical 
laboratory settings. The MeMed BV FlexTM test is indicated for use in testing samples from patients 
presenting with suspected acute bacterial or viral infection, who have had symptoms for less than seven 
days. The MeMed BV FlexTM test generates a numeric score that falls within discrete interpretation bins 
based on the increasing likelihood of bacterial infection.

8.2 Proposed limitations 
The test is indicated for patients presenting with suspected acute bacterial or viral infection who have

• Chronic fungal or parasitic infection .

12.3 Protocol deviations 
A protocol deviation is noncompliance with the clinical study protocol and GCP requirements. The 
noncompliance may be either on the part of the participant, the investigator, or the study site staff. As 
a result of deviations, corrective actions are to be developed by the site and implemented promptly. 
These practices are consistent with ICH E6 . 
It is the responsibility of the site to use continuous vigilance to identify and report deviations. All 
deviations must be addressed in study source documents. Protocol deviations may be sent to the local 
IRB per their guidelines. The site PI/study staff is responsible for knowing and adhering to their IRB 
requirements.   
12.4 Conflict of interest policy 
The independence of this study from any actual or perceived influence is critical. Therefore, any actual 
conflict of interest of persons who have a role in the design, conduct, analysis, publication, or any aspect 
of this study will be disclosed and managed. Furthermore, persons who have a perceived conflict of 
interest will be required to have such conflicts managed in a way that is appropriate to their participation 
in the study. The study leadership has established policies and procedures for all study group members 
to disclose all conflicts of interest and will establish a mechanism for the management of all reported 
dualities of interest.
