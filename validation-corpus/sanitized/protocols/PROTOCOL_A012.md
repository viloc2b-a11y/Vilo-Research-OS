Protocol Title:  
A Phase 2, Multicenter, Randomized, Observer-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study to Evaluate the 
Safety and Immunogenicity of [PROTOCOL_A012] Vaccine in Seronegative Women 18-40 Years of Age 
at Risk for Primary CMV Infection 
 
Short Title:  
A Phase 2 Study to Evaluate Safety and Immunogenicity of [PROTOCOL_A012] Vaccine  
in Seronegative Women at Risk for Primary CMV Infection 
 
Rationale: 
Young children who acquire CMV infection frequently shed high levels of virus in saliva and 
urine for several months [Cannon, 2014].  CMV-seronegative women who care for young 
children are at increased risk for acquiring primary CMV infection through contact with infected 
secretions.   At particular risk are CMV-seronegative women of childbearing age, as 
approximately 30-35% of women acquiring primary CMV infection during pregnancy give birth 
to infants with congenital CMV infection (cCMV) [Kenneson, 2007].   Additionally, fetal CMV 
infection is the cause of   
 
Approximately 10-15% of newborns with cCMV have signs or symptoms at birth, the most 
severe of which include microcephaly, chorioretinitis, seizures, jaundice, hepatosplenomegaly, 
and purpura, with a 10% mortality rate in the newborn period [Boppana, 1992].  Infants who 
are symptomatic at birth and survive the postnatal period can manifest a spectrum of long-
term sequelae including sensorineural hearing loss, developmental delay, microcephaly, 
cerebral palsy, and seizure disorders [Dollard 2007, Lopez 2014, Manicklal 2013; Smithers-
Sheedy 2017].  Of infants with cCMV who are asymptomatic at birth,  approximately half will 
suffer from neurocognitive sequelae such as sensorineural hearing loss and developmental 
delay  [Goderis 2014, Fowler 1999; Fowler 2006]. 
 
A vaccine against congenital human cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection is a major public health 
priority.  [SPONSOR_A], Inc. have developed a proprietary messenger RNA (mRNA)-based vaccine 
platform for intramuscular delivery of mRNA encoding target antigens in a lipid nanoparticle 
formulation.  Endogenous translation of the mRNA produces structurally-intact viral antigens 
that mimic those of a natural infection.  The [PROTOCOL_A012] vaccine against CMV infection consists 
of 6 distinct mRNA sequences encoding important targets of neutralizing antibody response to 
human CMV infection:  full length CMV glycoprotein B (gB) and pentameric 
gH/gL/UL128/UL130/UL131A glycoprotein complex (Pentamer).   [SPONSOR_A] is currently

Overall Design: This is an observer-blind, randomized, multi-center study envisaged to be conducted in 
multiple countries.  This study is designed to confirm safety and immunogenicity of the [PROTOCOL_A012] 
vaccine in CMV-seronegative women at risk for primary CMV infection through exposure to young 
children. The study is sized to accumulate 30 cases of primary CMV infection to support the secondary 
endpoint of prevention of infection with a case distribution of 1:3 [PROTOCOL_A012]:placebo recipients.   
Number of Participants: Approximately 825 subjects per arm (1,650 subjects total) will be enrolled.

Duration of the study: Each subject will be followed for 30 months (24 months after the third 
vaccination).   
Overview of Study Procedures:  Subjects will have approximately 13 clinic visits with periodic phone 
calls, emails or text messages between visits (Table 1).  During the vaccination period, safety 
assessments will be conducted by provision of a Diary Card to record daily local and systemic 
reactogenicity for the 7 days following each vaccination.  All adverse events (AEs) will be collected for 28 
days following each dose.  Serious adverse events (SAEs), Medically-Attended adverse events (MA-AE), 
adverse events of special interest (AESI) and any AE leading to withdrawal from dosing or study 
participation will be collected through the final study visit. Blood draws over the course of the study will 
assess for eligibility, immunogenicity to [PROTOCOL_A012] vaccine, or primary CMV infection.  Subjects will 
also submit urine and saliva samples at all study visits (Table 2).  At 5 timepoints during the post-
vaccination follow up period, subjects will be requested to submit a urine sample using a home 
collection kit and mail it to the testing laboratory.  Subjects of childbearing potential will be required to 
practice contraception through one month after the last vaccination.  If a subject becomes pregnant 
during the study, no further study vaccinations will be administered, the subject will continue with all 
other study procedures, and pregnancy outcome will be ascertained. 
 
Vaccination schedule:  A single 0.5mL dose will be administered on Day 1, Month 2, and Month 6. 
Study Vaccine:  [PROTOCOL_A012] is provided as a sterile liquid for injection at 0.5 mg/mL in 2-mL glass vials 
and stored at -20 ± 5ºC until use.   Final dose for this phase 2 study will be selected following evaluation 
of the current Phase 1, dose-ranging study with [PROTOCOL_A012].  Vaccine will be administered 
intramuscularly. 
Control:  Saline placebo. 
Randomization:  Subjects will be randomized 1:1 to receive either [PROTOCOL_A012] vaccine or placebo 
using an Interactive Web Response System (IWRS) and stratified according to country and clinical site.    
Blinding:  As the appearance of vaccine and placebo differs, the study will observer-blind as to 
treatment assignment.  
Data collection: Diary and electronic case report forms. 
Key Inclusion Criteria: 
1. Female, aged 18-40 at the time of first vaccination. 
2. Confirmed CMV seronegative status per protocol-specified testing. 
3. Is in good general health, as determined by medical history and physical examination. 
4. Has direct exposure (i.e., in the home or occupationally) to children  5 years of age.  Direct 
exposure is defined as either a) subject is the parent, or b) subject has close contact (feeding, 
diaper changes, supervision) for at least 8 hours per week. 
5. If of childbearing potential, agrees to avoid becoming pregnant from screening visit through 28 
days after the last vaccination by adhering to effective means of birth control (as specified in the 
protocol). 
 
Key Exclusion Criteria: 
1. Has a body mass index (BMI) that is ≥ 18 and   35 kg/m2.

Safety Oversight:  Safety monitoring will be primarily conducted by external medical monitors and a 
Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB).  
 
Statistical Considerations:  Approximately 1,650 subjects will be enrolled with 1:1 randomization ratio 
to [PROTOCOL_A012] or placebo. Given the target vaccine efficacy of at least 70% and the annual rate of 
primary infection at 1.5% (3.0% over the course of the study), the sample size of 1,650 will provide 
approximately 90% statistical power with 1-sided alpha of 0.05.   The number and rate of primary CMV 
infections will drive the unblinded case distribution analyses and will inform recruitment projections. 
An intent to treat (ITT) analysis will evaluate the ability of [PROTOCOL_A012] to prevent primary CMV infection 
using all cases occurring after one month from the 3rd vaccination.  A modified intent to treat (MITT) 
analysis will evaluate the ability of [PROTOCOL_A012] to prevent primary CMV infection using all cases 
occurring after one month from the 2nd vaccination.
