# Laboratory Governance Intelligence

## 1. Document Hierarchy
The intelligence engine must recognize the strict hierarchy of governance documents. They are not interchangeable.

1. **The Protocol:** Defines *WHAT* to collect and *WHEN* (The scientific justification).
2. **The Lab Manual:** Defines *HOW* to collect, process, and ship (The operational execution).
3. **The Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP):** Defines *HOW TO CALCULate* data despite deviations.

### Governance Conflict Resolution
- **Conflict:** Protocol says "Centrifuge for 10 mins", Lab Manual says "Centrifuge for 15 mins".
- **Resolution:** Operational execution follows the Lab Manual. The discrepancy must be queried to the Sponsor for a global document update, but the Lab Manual dictates the physical handling.

## 2. Authority Matrix
- **Site PI:** Ultimate authority over *Patient Safety*. Can refuse to redraw blood if deemed unsafe.
- **Sponsor:** Ultimate authority over *Data Viability*. Decides if a deviated sample is tested or destroyed.
- **Central Lab:** Ultimate authority over *Analytical Viability*. Rejects samples that physically break the testing machine (e.g., massive clotting).

## 3. Regulatory Reporting (FDA/IRB)
Not every dropped test tube is reported to the FDA.
- **Immediate IRB Reporting (Unanticipated Problem):** Only required if the deviation increased risk to the subject (e.g., severe hemorrhage during collection).
- **Routine Deviation Reporting:** Logged in the eCRF/CTMS and reviewed during monitoring. Included in the final Clinical Study Report (CSR).
