# Finding Prevention Intelligence

## 1. Overview
A "Finding" occurs when a CRA visits a site and discovers a systemic or procedural error in how the study is being executed. VIP's goal is to detect these vulnerabilities and force internal correction prior to the Interim Monitoring Visit (IMV).

## 2. Prevention Vectors

### The "Batch Signing" Finding
- **What the CRA will see:** PI signed 30 Central Lab reports on the same day, 4 weeks after the labs were drawn.
- **VIP Defense:** "15 Unsigned Lab Reports pending PI Review. Safety Oversight Risk. Probability of Major Finding: 90%. Action: Auto-escalate urgent task to PI dashboard; warn Site Director."

### The "Delegation/Training Gap" Finding
- **What the CRA will see:** Nurse A administered IP on Monday, but GCP training expired on Sunday.
- **VIP Defense:** "Nurse A GCP expiring in 7 days. Action: VIP revokes Nurse A's system permissions to administer IP on day 8 until new certificate is uploaded. Major deviation prevented."

### The "Unresolved Discrepancy" Finding
- **What the CRA will see:** Subject weight was 80kg at Visit 1, and 50kg at Visit 2. No comment in source.
- **VIP Defense:** "37% weight drop detected. Biological implausibility. Probability of CRA Finding: 99%. Action: Prompt CRC to verify scale reading or add 'Patient amputated/dehydrated' source note prior to saving."

## 3. Site Impact
Preventing findings shields the Principal Investigator (PI) from embarrassing and legally risky Sponsor Warning Letters, keeping the site in good standing for future trial selection.
