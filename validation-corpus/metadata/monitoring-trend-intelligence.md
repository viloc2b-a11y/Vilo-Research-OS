# Monitoring Trend Intelligence

## 1. Overview
A single error is human. A trend of errors is a broken system. VIP is now trained to distinguish isolated slip-ups from systemic risk signals.

## 2. The Trend Evaluation Matrix

### A. Missing Timestamps
- **Single Event (1x):** Human error. Addressed via Query.
- **Pattern (10x):** Carelessness. Addressed via CRA retraining the coordinator.
- **System Failure (50x):** Broken process. **Action:** Formal CAPA required. The clinic's clocks might be broken, or the worksheet design is flawed.

### B. Processing Delays
- **Single Event (1x):** Lab was busy. Deviation logged.
- **Pattern (5x):** Understaffing. CRA warns Site Manager.
- **System Failure (30x):** Critical Site Risk Signal. **Action:** Halt enrollment. The site fundamentally lacks the infrastructure or staff to execute the protocol.

### C. Overdue Queries
- **Single Event (1x):** Coordinator missed an email.
- **Pattern (20x):** Poor time management.
- **System Failure (200x):** Coordinator Overload Signal. **Action:** Sponsor Risk Escalation. The site took on too many trials. Data integrity is collapsing. Payments frozen.

## 3. FDA/Sponsor View of Trends
The FDA does not expect perfection. They expect **Detection and Correction**.
If the Sponsor (via the CRA) detects a trend but fails to implement a CAPA to fix it, the Sponsor is cited for "Failure to Monitor". Therefore, Sponsors will aggressively penalize sites that exhibit trends to protect their own regulatory standing.
